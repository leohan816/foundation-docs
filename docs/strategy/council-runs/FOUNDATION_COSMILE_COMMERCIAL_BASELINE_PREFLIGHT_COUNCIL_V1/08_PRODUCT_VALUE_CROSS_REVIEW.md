AGREED_FINDINGS:

1. All three initial reports support `PROCEED_WITH_CORRECTIONS`, not audit activation as written. A current commercial decision baseline is useful because the present as-built flows, evidence boundary, blocker inventory, estimates, and external readiness remain unresolved.
2. Three working days is a maximum for bounded evidence collection and package synthesis, not a promise of product readiness, production certification, legal/privacy/security closure, external readiness, implementation, or exhaustive review.
3. The audit needs a staged structure: frozen admission controls, a Day 1 decision gate, first-decision-relevant or first-blocking-boundary tracing, a static-first evidence ceiling, an evidence/package freeze, and no automatic extension.
4. The eighteen output concerns should not become eighteen independently authored deliverables. Delivery's P1–P5 structure is a workable consolidation if it is implemented as sections or generated views over one authoritative row set rather than five new documentation projects.
5. Audit completion must be independent from Paid Beta READY and Public Launch READY. A useful audit may close with product gates `NO`, `HOLD`, or `UNVERIFIED`.
6. Foundation and SIASIU must be classified only in relation to the selected beta path. The audit must not assume either platform is a mandatory commercial dependency, and it must not expand into Foundation architecture, Memory V3 implementation, or a full SIASIU review.
7. Ordinary-commerce continuity and AI consultation closed/hidden behavior are material gate questions. The required evidence fields belong in selected critical-flow rows; unresolved evidence may hold readiness without preventing audit closure.
8. The evidence model should use E0–E4 for technical evidence and a separate external-state dimension. Static-only is the correct default, with at most a narrow preauthorized E3 exception when it can change the implementation-priority decision.
9. Every READY label, branch recommendation, external non-requirement, and follow-up recommendation must carry output-local non-authorization. Evidence classification is not product selection, risk acceptance, release approval, branch authority, dispatch, or next-mission authority.
10. Founder admission requirements must be smaller than the Preflight's six-decision list. Authorization, safety, decision envelope, known non-negotiables, and authority semantics belong before dispatch; final product option, branch, implementation, architecture, risk, release, and substantive external decisions should be informed by evidence.
11. Blocker classification needs a direct customer/operator and selected-gate causality test. General debt and out-of-slice unknowns must not become Paid Beta blockers merely because they are technically incomplete.
12. Estimates must be limited to the recommended beta option and must separate active engineering effort from external elapsed time, parallel work, and confidence.

REASON_FOR_DISAGREEMENT:

The remaining differences are about when a control must exist and how broadly it applies, not whether the reserved risks matter. From the product/user/business lens, pre-activation requirements should authorize and bound investigation; they should not demand the evidence or product decisions that the investigation exists to produce. Audit-row requirements should apply to the selected beta slice and only when they can change a priority, gate, or reserved-risk conclusion. Later release requirements may remain strict without making the baseline audit open-ended. This timing and slice distinction preserves safety while avoiding another authority-document and platform-completeness loop.

REBUTTALS:

- **To the claim that absence of continuity evidence must block the audit:** It must block an affected READY claim, not the collection and closure of the baseline. The pre-activation requirement is the field, evidence ceiling, and non-bypass rule. If static evidence ends at `UNVERIFIED`, the audit can close with a precise release hold and no extension.
- **To a full governance-register interpretation:** Canonical owner, writer/reader, identity/consent authority, PII class, retention/deletion/auditability status, and owner belong only on selected critical-flow rows. Unknown Legal/privacy facts remain owner-pinned unresolved records. The audit does not determine lawful basis, approve policy, design storage, or inspect unrelated surfaces.
- **To putting P5 closure inside Day 3 by default:** Independent review and Advisor completeness closure are valuable precisely because they are not rushed synthesis by the investigators. Their time must be reserved and measured, but forcing them into the investigative cap can reduce evidence quality or independence. The claim should be “three investigative working days plus separately measured closure” unless capacity is explicitly included.
- **To requiring all external owner names before admission:** Naming is useful, but an unknown owner is itself decision-relevant audit output. It should become an admission blocker only if the audit plan depends on that person's response to select the priority; otherwise it closes `EXTERNAL_PENDING` and blocks only the affected readiness or external decision.
- **To allowing new E4 because a safe command may exist:** Integration evidence is valuable for release confidence, but the commercial-baseline decision can close at E1/E2 plus targeted E3. New E4 work consumes scarce time and risks turning priority reconstruction into validation. It belongs in a later, selected-slice validation step after the implementation priority is chosen.
- **To treating eighteen concerns as completeness requirements:** They are useful questions and views, not equal-depth work obligations. Any question that cannot change the selected beta option, top blocker, estimate, or reserved-risk boundary should be marked `SAFE_TO_DEFER` rather than chased.

### CR-01 — Minimum sufficient process

I support the shared formulation with these exact changes:

**Day 0 / admission** must freeze one compact manifest containing: subject/repository pins; recommendation-only authority and return route; selected roles, package integrator, capacity windows, and cutoffs; static-first evidence ceiling; exact optional E3 allowlist if any; the product decision envelope and known non-negotiables/exclusions; frozen decision questions; authoritative-row/P1–P5 map; evidence and package freeze; drift, unsafe-access, capacity, commercial-frame, scope, and expiry stops; and output-local non-authorization. Day 0 must not choose the product, branch, architecture, risk, or release.

**Day 1** must produce at least one bounded beta option card with target user/problem hypothesis, intended outcome/learning, transaction or consultation mode, operator assumption, minimum slice, Foundation/SIASIU dependency classifications at the available evidence ceiling, and the decision the option would unlock. Facts, inference, assumptions, and unknowns remain separate. A Day 1 option is an investigation frame, not a Founder product selection.

Use these predicates:

- `CONTINUE`: at least one credible option exists; one or more named unknowns can materially change the recommended priority, blocker, estimate, or reserved-risk conclusion; those questions fit the remaining safe time/capacity; and no stop condition applies.
- `EARLY_COMPLETE`: current pinned evidence is already sufficient to recommend a next implementation or customer-learning priority, and every frozen question is evidenced, explicitly unresolved, or documented as unable to change that recommendation. `EARLY_COMPLETE` may recommend `NOT_NEEDED`; it authorizes no work.
- `HOLD`: no credible option can be formed; the decision requires prohibited evidence or an unbounded expansion; pins/authority/capacity fail; a critical evidence-integrity conflict occurs; or the package cannot distinguish a next priority within the fixed timebox.

**Days 2–3** may trace only decision-changing questions to the first decision-relevant/blocking boundary, populate the authoritative rows continuously, synthesize P1–P4, and freeze without extension. After freeze, P5 Independent Reviewer and Advisor closure is separately measured unless admission explicitly reserves enough Day 3 capacity for it.

### CR-03 — Demand and customer-learning threshold

A validated or committed cohort is **not** an admission prerequisite. The exact minimum is:

- At admission: a named decision objective and permission to test a bounded option envelope, plus any known non-negotiables. Missing market facts remain unknown.
- By the Day 1 gate: at least one testable provisional option naming a target-user/problem hypothesis, intended value or learning, transaction/consultation mode, operator assumption, minimum customer-facing slice, and the choice that further evidence could change. Each element must be labeled fact, inference, assumption, or unknown.
- `HOLD`: no option meets that threshold, or every option depends on prohibited/unbounded investigation.
- `NOT_NEEDED`: either an equivalent current pinned baseline already supports the decision, or a defined customer-learning-first step is materially cheaper, can run without the unresolved technical baseline, and has an owner, timebox, success/stop signal, and explicit result-to-next-priority rule. A hypothetical “talk to customers” suggestion alone is insufficient.

The audit may formulate bounded options on Day 1; doing so is not a product decision. It may not invent demand, market readiness, willingness to pay, or cohort commitment.

- `FOUNDATION_DEPENDENCY: REQUIRED | OPTIONAL_DEGRADABLE | NOT_REQUIRED | UNVERIFIED`.

- `SIASIU_DEPENDENCY: MANDATORY | OPTIONAL | ABSENT_AT_VERIFIED_BOUNDARY | UNVERIFIED`.
- Technical evidence level, exact pin, last verified boundary, failure effect, evidence ceiling, and affected gate.
- Only for selected critical data/judgment flows: canonical owner/system of record, authorized writer/reader, identity boundary, consent authority, PII class, and retention/deletion/auditability status. Each may be `VERIFIED`, `NOT_APPLICABLE_BY_NAMED_OWNER`, or `UNVERIFIED` with owner and gate impact.

### CR-05 — Operational evidence and closure predicates

**Technical evidence** remains:

- `E0_REPORTED`: historical prose or owner report; investigation lead only.
- `E1_GIT_IDENTIFIED`: exact current repo/commit/blob/path; source/artifact existence only.
- `E2_STATIC_CONNECTED`: pinned entry/config/call/schema/contract chain to the last static boundary; no execution claim.
- `E3_LOCAL_EXECUTION_VERIFIED`: exact preauthorized hermetic local scenario, current pin, command, result, timestamp, isolated generated data, cleanup, and no prohibited resource.
- `E4_INTEGRATION_VERIFIED`: authorized isolated non-production end-to-end scenario and failures. Existing current pinned E4 evidence may be recorded, but no new E4 execution belongs in this baseline audit.

Record external readiness separately as `EXTERNAL_VERIFIED | EXTERNAL_PENDING | NOT_REQUIRED_BY_NAMED_OWNER | OWNER_UNASSIGNED`. External status never raises the technical evidence level.

Use these predicates:

- `DAY3_PACKAGE_COMPLETE`: every frozen row has status, technical level, exact pin/last boundary, external state, decision impact, and either closure evidence or a complete unresolved record; P1–P4 are frozen; product diff is zero; no boundary violation occurred.
- `AUDIT_COMPLETE`: `DAY3_PACKAGE_COMPLETE: YES` plus P5 immutable package pins, Independent Reviewer result, Advisor completeness/evidence closure, unresolved disagreements, and return route. It may occur after the three investigative days and never implies readiness or approval.
- `PAID_BETA_BLOCKER`: a confirmed item tied to the selected beta option and gate, with a direct customer/operator consequence, failure if deferred, exact critical-flow pin, sufficient evidence at the declared ceiling, no value-preserving workaround inside the approved slice, minimum closure evidence, and deferral destination. An unverified risk may be `PAID_BETA_GATE_HOLD_UNVERIFIED`, but it is not mislabeled a confirmed blocker.
- `PAID_BETA_READY`: evidence-only classification for one exact option/slice. Every in-slice critical flow meets its predeclared minimum evidence; no confirmed blocker or gate-holding unknown remains; continuity/dependency and selected-flow governance requirements are satisfied; necessary external states are verified or signed not-required by the proper owner. This authorizes no release.
- `PUBLIC_LAUNCH_READY`: a separately evidenced classification against the frozen Public Launch delta. Paid Beta evidence may be referenced but cannot be treated as launch evidence without proving the broader exposure/scale/operations requirements. This authorizes no release.

Every unresolved row must include exact question, attempted/available evidence, reason unresolved, owner or `OWNER_UNASSIGNED`, selected option/gate, user/business and decision impact, minimum resolving evidence, earliest external date if known, and disposition `HOLDS_PRIORITY_DECISION | HOLDS_RELEASE_GATE | CHANGES_ESTIMATE | SAFE_TO_DEFER`. Complete unresolved rows close without extension.

Every READY, option, branch, external non-requirement, or next-step record must state:

```text
EVIDENCE_CLASSIFICATION_ONLY: YES
SELECTED_BY_LEO: NONE
RISK_ACCEPTED: NONE
ACTION_AUTHORIZED: NONE
RELEASE_AUTHORIZED: NO
NEXT_MISSION_AUTHORIZED: NO
```

An external `NOT_REQUIRED` additionally needs the exact named owner, authority basis, scope, and date; otherwise use `EXTERNAL_PENDING` or `OWNER_UNASSIGNED`.
