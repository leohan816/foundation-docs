# Role-Specific Correction Review Questions

## PRODUCT_USER_AND_BUSINESS_VALUE_CHALLENGER

- Does Foundation-first have a concrete decision outcome, or can it still become platform
  work for its own sake?
- Does the P1 selection rule prevent deepening capabilities merely because they might have
  future consumers?
- Will one evidence-derived candidate give Leo a useful next-priority decision?
- Is there a simpler Foundation-only baseline that preserves the same decision value?

Authorized report: `10_PRODUCT_VALUE_CORRECTION_REVIEW.md`.

## SYSTEM_ARCHITECTURE_SAFETY_AND_GOVERNANCE_CHALLENGER

- Are canonical mission, physical location, authority, ownership, runtime, and target fit
  separated correctly?
- Is the historical `foundation-control` boundary safe?
- Is the downstream-evidence exception narrow enough to prevent cross-project scope drift?
- Are memory, learning, canonical writes, and future matching prevented from becoming
  hidden activation or architecture decisions?
- Is the post-evidence gate sufficient for a candidate that might later change ownership,
  data, AI behavior, or governance?

Authorized report: `11_SYSTEMS_RISK_CORRECTION_REVIEW.md`.

## DELIVERY_SCOPE_EVIDENCE_AND_OPERATIONS_CHALLENGER

- Can the stated census, probes, review, and six-artifact closure be completed within a
  bounded estimate?
- Are exact Git/command/environment/output/failure/cleanup evidence requirements practical?
- Are representative and negative probe classes frozen early enough to prevent cherry-picking?
- Are the material-duration and scope-deviation returns measurable?
- Does one non-executable candidate avoid both option sprawl and premature implementation?

Authorized report: `12_DELIVERY_EVIDENCE_CORRECTION_REVIEW.md`.

## DATA_QUALITY_LINEAGE_AND_GOVERNANCE_CHALLENGER

- Does the contract adequately trace canonical source, provenance, version, transformation,
  index inclusion, retrieval, reasoning/judgment use, and response evidence?
- Are atomic-product, matching, rights, correction, retraction, and stewardship claim
  ceilings sufficiently constrained?
- Can missingness, conflict, duplication, freshness, coverage, and derived-data lineage be
  assessed without silently designing a new schema?
- Does P4 prevent evidence-derived recommendations from silently redefining canonical data?

Authorized report: `13_DATA_GOVERNANCE_CORRECTION_REVIEW.md`.

## AI_MODEL_BEHAVIOR_AND_EVALUATION_CHALLENGER

- Are deterministic logic, model/provider responsibility, routing, prompts/context,
  fallback, judgment, safety, evidence, memory, and tool authority separated?
- Are ordinary, missing, conflicting, stale, unsafe, unavailable, fallback, and degraded
  probe classes sufficient for exact observations?
- Does the contract prevent small samples and successful traces from becoming general
  reliability or production claims?
- Are model/provider execution and future learning held behind adequate authority and
  claim ceilings?

Authorized report: `14_AI_BEHAVIOR_CORRECTION_REVIEW.md`.
