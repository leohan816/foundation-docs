# Revised Foundation-Only Decision Contract

```text
STATUS: CORRECTION_REVIEW_SUBJECT
AUTHORITY: NON_EXECUTABLE_STRATEGY_CONTRACT
CURRENT_PRIORITY: FOUNDATION_ITSELF_FIRST
ADVISOR_DISPATCHED: NO
BASELINE_STARTED: NO
IMPLEMENTATION_AUTHORIZED: NO
MEMORY_V3: PAUSED
```

## Primary decision question

How much of Foundation itself is actually implemented, buildable, tested,
runtime-demonstrated, integrated, and governed today?

Determine:

- which Foundation capabilities truly work;
- which are source-only, mock, shadow, partial, historical, dead, blocked, or unverified;
- which canonical knowledge, retrieval, model, judgment, safety, evidence, API, runtime,
  and memory components exist;
- how far Foundation has progressed toward its own current canonical mission;
- what should be retained, repaired, boundedly transferred, replaced, retired, deferred,
  or investigated further;
- what one next bounded Foundation implementation priority should be considered after
  evidence and governance review.

## Current outcome envelope

The factual baseline evaluates Foundation itself:

- canonical knowledge core and provenance;
- product, brand, ingredient, claim, warning, safety, evidence, and source structure;
- atomic product representation at `STRUCTURAL_READINESS_ONLY` claim ceiling;
- retrieval, index, vector, graph, lexical, hybrid, embedding, and reranking capabilities;
- model, provider, routing, fallback, prompt/context, and evaluation behavior;
- deterministic logic, judgment, verdict, safety, and evidence composition;
- API packaging, runtime entry points, failure behavior, restart, and observability;
- internal memory-state and learning-control map without activation;
- operational ownership, authority, governance, correction, retraction, rollback, and
  stewardship readiness at exact evidence ceilings.

Foundation's current canonical mission must be pinned from current canonical Foundation
documents during P0/P1. Strategy, Advisor, or Council may not invent a future architecture
and then grade current code against it.

Longer-term atomic matching and governed learning direction may be assessed only as
`TARGET_FIT`, `STRUCTURAL_READINESS_ONLY`, or `SOURCE_AND_CONTROL_READINESS_ONLY`.
No person schema, matching policy, consent policy, autonomous learning, promotion,
canonical write, or Memory V3 activation is authorized.

## Explicit non-objectives

SIASIU development is not a current near-term objective. Cosmile AI development is not a
current objective. The factual baseline must not use SIASIU consultation or Cosmile AI as
the target architecture, consumer requirement, or reason to deepen a Foundation path.

Do not:

- begin or design SIASIU implementation;
- begin or design Cosmile AI implementation;
- modify Cosmile Golden Commerce;
- make Foundation a synchronous dependency of ordinary commerce;
- activate Memory V3, sender/intake, autonomous jobs, promotion, or serving mutation;
- begin Foundation implementation;
- modify product code, canonical knowledge, schema, or shared data.

## Targeted downstream evidence gate

Foundation-local evidence is always examined first. SIASIU or Cosmile may be inspected
only as targeted read-only downstream evidence when an exact Foundation question cannot
otherwise determine current consumption, API/contract reality, integration status, or
failure/degraded behavior.

Before any downstream inspection, P1 must record:

```text
DOWNSTREAM_QUESTION_ID
EXACT_FOUNDATION_QUESTION
WHY_FOUNDATION_LOCAL_EVIDENCE_IS_INSUFFICIENT
DECISION_IMPACT
EXACT_REPOSITORY_AND_PATH_BOUNDARY
EXACT_ACTOR_IF_REQUIRED
TIMEBOX
COLLISION_STATE
PROHIBITED_DEPTH
STOP_CONDITION
```

No downstream Worker is selected by default. If the gate is not satisfied, close the
item as `NOT_REQUIRED_FOR_DECISION`, `DEFERRED`, `UNVERIFIED`, or `OUT_OF_SCOPE`.

## Unknown disposition

Factual unknowns may not be resolved by Council reasoning. Use only:

- `RESOLVED_BY_COUNCIL_ANALYSIS` for strategy, scope, architecture, prioritization, and
  risk reasoning;
- `RESOLVED_BY_REPOSITORY_EVIDENCE`;
- `RESOLVED_BY_AUTHORITATIVE_SOURCE`;
- `LEO_DECISION_REQUIRED`;
- `VENDOR_CONFIRMATION_REQUIRED`;
- `LEGAL_OR_ACCOUNTING_COUNSEL_REQUIRED`;
- `IMPLEMENTATION_VALIDATION_REQUIRED`;
- `OUT_OF_SCOPE`.

Each unknown records ID, question, classification, significance, evidence, owner,
resolution method, required timing, blocking effect, and final disposition.

## P0 — Admission, canonical mission pin, and bounded estimate

Before product-repository execution:

- verify exact repository, branch, HEAD, upstream, worktree, authority, actor capacity,
  active mission, and collision state;
- pin the current canonical Foundation mission and exact canonical sources;
- preserve unrelated existing changes;
- freeze exclusions, evidence vocabulary, claim ceilings, and no-build boundary;
- inventory candidate commands and risk tiers;
- define isolated temporary resources, networks, providers, credentials-by-name only,
  cost caps, abort owners, and cleanup;
- return a finite workday and elapsed-time estimate, confidence, critical path, and
  material-deviation threshold.

P0 result: `CONTINUE | NARROW | HOLD`.

The mission returns to Strategy/Leo if the projected duration exceeds the P0 upper bound
by more than `max(1 working day, 25%)`, or if a new repository, actor class, provider
authority, data/risk class, product/canonical write, ownership decision, or material scope
is required.

## P1 — Foundation-wide shallow census and probe freeze

Create a broad, shallow, Foundation-first physical and authority map before deep probes.
It includes:

- repository, package, dependency, configuration, test, CI, runtime, and ownership surfaces;
- canonical Vault/knowledge identity, provenance, version, rights status, correction,
  retraction, stewardship, transformations, and derived indexes;
- product atomicity, identifiers, aliases, semantics, units, locale/market/time,
  relationships, missingness, conflict, duplication, freshness, and coverage denominators;
- retrieval/index, model/routing, deterministic logic, judgment, safety, evidence, API,
  memory, learning-control, runtime, operations, and observability surfaces;
- exact relevant historical `foundation-control` physical implementation;
- current consumers and explicit non-consumption without treating them as objectives.

Freeze representative probe classes before execution. They must include, where relevant:

- ordinary successful behavior;
- missing knowledge;
- conflicting knowledge;
- stale or retracted knowledge;
- unsafe or unsupported questions;
- unavailable index, route, model, or provider;
- fallback and degraded behavior;
- provenance and evidence reaching the final response;
- restart, recovery, and cleanup behavior.

Independent Reviewer challenges the frozen protocol without coauthoring it. P1 decides
which slices are decision-relevant and safely admissible. Do not deepen a path merely for
target-architecture completeness or because a downstream product might use it later.

## P2 — Selected isolated Foundation verification

Execute only P1-selected slices in a mission-owned isolated non-production environment.
Allowed, when safely admitted:

- existing build and tests;
- local API execution;
- retrieval/index execution;
- synthetic-question and controlled synthetic conflict/ablation probes;
- model/provider execution only with separately verified authority and bounded cost;
- memory-boundary and learning-control inspection without mutation or activation.

Each probe records exact pins, commands, principal, reads/writes, temporary paths,
dependencies, listeners, networks, provider/account/environment, credential names only,
synthetic inputs, request/token/time/cost caps, evidence, abort owner, teardown, and
before/after resource inventory.

Missing prerequisites yield `NOT_RUN`, `UNVERIFIED`, or `PROVIDER_EXECUTION_NOT_RUN`.
Boundary breach, secret/PII exposure, shared/production contact, public listener,
canonical mutation, Memory/promotion activation, cost overrun, or cleanup failure yields
immediate `HOLD` for the affected phase.

For stochastic behavior, repetitions and sample claims must be justified before execution;
small samples prove only exact observations, never general reliability or production safety.

## P3 — Historical and strictly conditional downstream evidence

Inspect relevant historical `foundation-control` source as physical and prior-behavior
evidence only. It grants no current Worker authority, canonical ownership, transfer,
deprecation, or implementation authority.

Use the targeted downstream evidence gate above only when needed to answer an exact
Foundation question. No complete downstream audit, feature design, or implementation is
allowed.

## P4 — Evidence freeze, Independent Review, and non-executable candidate

1. Freeze content-addressed current-state evidence.
2. Independent Reviewer inspects actual code, canonical sources, commands, configuration,
   tests, runtime probes, traces, failures, unknown routing, boundary compliance, and cleanup.
3. Responsible evidence owners may apply bounded factual/document corrections only;
   changed evidence is re-frozen and re-reviewed as required.
4. After reviewed evidence, P4 may produce exactly one evidence-derived bounded Foundation
   implementation candidate.
5. The candidate must record exact evidence/unknown IDs, problem and outcome, scope,
   excluded scope, owner, dependencies, reversibility, test requirements, estimate,
   confidence, alternatives, and unresolved Leo/external decisions.

The P4 candidate must state:

```text
STATUS: NON_EXECUTABLE
IMPLEMENTATION_AUTHORIZED: NO
PENDING_POST_EVIDENCE_COUNCIL_AND_LEO_DECISION: YES
ADVISOR_IMPLEMENTATION_DISPATCHED: NO
```

After Advisor final audit, the factual baseline and candidate return to Strategy. Strategy
must route the evidence-based candidate through the previously approved post-evidence
Council challenge before final Strategy synthesis and Leo decision. No implementation,
activation, or next mission starts automatically.
