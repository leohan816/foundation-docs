REBUTTALS:
- To “one trace proves the backbone”: it proves bounded connectivity and selected state handling only. Naming it rehearsal with a walking-skeleton ceiling preserves value without promoting reliability, live safety, or commercial meaning.
- To “captured refund overbuilds the first reversal”: a void before capture does not exercise the economic correction that makes reversal decision-critical. One sandbox captured-refund case is the narrow minimum; post-shipment return and other policy cases remain exposure-driven.
- To “all contracts must be frozen before planning”: a planning-only mission exists to produce safe options and contracts. Strategy must freeze invariant categories and decision gates, while provider-specific and field-level contracts remain outputs that cannot authorize implementation.
- To “a disposable spike is always required”: Strategy cannot authorize product execution. A spike is justified only after permitted planning evidence leaves a decision-critical runtime question, and it requires separate Leo authority, predeclared failure criteria, cleanup, and a non-live evidence ceiling.
- To “operator assistance is inadequate for beta”: assistance can be safe for a bounded cohort when the path is disclosed, reachable, authorized, auditable, timely, legally sufficient, and evidenced. Requiring full self-service would import Public Launch scope; omitting both assisted and self-service recovery would be unsafe.
- To “all Strategy requirements should be encoded as detailed matrices”: detailed matrices are necessary planning artifacts but excessive Strategy detail would pre-decide provider, Legal, security, data, and UX choices without facts. Strategy should specify required columns/categories and closure authority, not fill them.
