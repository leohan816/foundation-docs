## Product finding and governing principles

The current candidate is technically strong but the real default viewport is a
debug-like spatial dashboard: oversized actor cards dominate the scene, actors
cluster, text truncates, relationship lines create noise, Team space is unclear,
and the product does not immediately read as a living AI organization.

This is an in-scope Batch A correction, not post-approval polish.

1. The product experience is the contract.
2. The UI is not a projection of the test suite; tests project the intended
   product experience.
3. User experience is the goal and tests protect it.
4. Hierarchy: `Space -> Behavior -> Information -> Technology`.
5. Living Office remains primary; Technical Dashboard remains secondary.
6. Review begins with the product, not implementation reports.
7. These principles are later canonicalized in Batch A-2 as Founder UX
   Principle 001; A-2 implementation is not authorized here.

## Spatial and behavior contract

- One shared floor contains multiple spatially separated, registry-derived
  Advisor Team Pods; one floor does not mean one actor cluster.
- Do not create an Agent Office Pod without a registered Agent Office Advisor.
- Team identity uses short text, optional configured mark, restrained accent,
  Mission Board motif, and limited props; never full-room saturated fills.
- Advisor gets a Mission Board/coordination desk; Control an architecture/design
  surface; each Worker an individual desk; Designer a design board when present;
  Reviewer an independent booth/zone; idle/reference actors a quiet peripheral
  seat.
- Every actor has a distinct position. Proximity never creates authority.
- Use a coherent 45-degree/3/4 perspective with square/diamond floor tiles,
  angled furniture/monitors, and consistent depth/occlusion.
- Evidence-backed behavior precedes technical information. Unknown state is
  neutral, never active-work animation.
- Worker, Control, Reviewer, Advisor, and future Designer poses must express
  only accepted role/state evidence. No celebratory review pose precedes a
  verified verdict. Monitor motion exposes no secret, source, or unsupported
  exact activity.

## Information and interaction contract

Default actor label contains only stable display name, role, concise critical
state, Team accent, and short textual Team identifier. Color is never the only
Team signal.

Critical states remain directly visible: session offline, no AI process, AI
error, blocked, needs Leo, operational unknown, authority/security hold, and
decision-critical conflict. Metadata unknowns may be summarized compactly.

Desktop hover/focus quick card shows current mission, WorkUnit, model, effort,
blocker/Leo state, KST last-verified time, and concise evidenced runtime. It
auto-places without covering the actor or essential furniture.

Click pins one compact card with hover facts plus runtime, progress, next action,
reports-to, and `상세 보기`. Exactly one card may be pinned; another actor
replaces it; empty-space click and Escape close it.

The full drawer preserves complete accepted actor facts and provenance.

Mobile uses `Tap actor -> compact bottom sheet -> 상세 보기 -> full drawer`.
Another actor replaces content; empty Office closes it. Relationship lines are
hidden by default and appear only for explicit actor/Team/relationship selection.

## Channy, semantics, and deferred capabilities

Channy must be a recognizable Bedlington Terrier with appropriate scale,
placement, walk/patrol, sniff, head tilt, light scratch/curiosity, eat, drink,
sleep/rest, and affectionate ambient poses. Channy has no authority and cannot
classify interruption, send tmux input, continue, retry, recover, approve, or
handle capacity/billing/credential/DB/production/Git risk.

The complete semantic equivalent remains. In ordinary visual mode, the large
actor list is collapsed behind `접근 가능한 에이전트 목록 (N)` while screen
reader parity remains. The list becomes primary for static/no-canvas,
high-text, renderer failure, or explicit accessibility mode. Reduced motion and
non-color meaning remain mandatory.

Create a deferred capability register for role animation, runtime-driven
animation, dynamic admission, real Advisor delivery, live status response,
handoff movement, completion choreography, recovery/Channy watchdog, bounded
retry, and future opt-in ambient sound. Every row records current level,
deferred batch/reason, early risk, prerequisites, owner, activation condition,
forbidden current behavior, tests/evidence, and final authority. A-1R activates
only role-specific basic presentation; Batch B-E and E-2 remain gated.
